PAAC Day-Patient Model

Go-Live: 1 April 2026

As mandated by COC(ANA), the Pre-Anaesthetic Assessment Clinic is transitioning from Outpatient to a Day-patient model to align with best practices and improve same-day coordination.

  • ✔️ CMS Action: Select "Discharge Summary".
  • ✔️ Template: Use PAAC new 2026 version.
  • ✔️ Physiotherapy: Arrange in-patient physio referral for same afternoon consultation.
  • ✔️ Medications: Patients should bring usual meds. Can prescribe in IPMOE if forgotten.

🚨 Clinical Incidents in Day Ward

For unusual incidents (e.g., symptomatic severe hypertension BP > 200, chest pain):

Urgent Contact medical team by phone immediately.
Non-Urgent Contact parent team to arrange admission for management.

Bariatric Surgery ICU Audit & Proposal

Audit of 82 cases (Jan 2023 - Nov 2025) reviewed. 10 admitted to ICU. Findings showed no significant post-op respiratory complications; most stayed 1 day with no re-intubation.

Proposed New Criteria (Pilot Study)

Aiming to optimize ICU bed utilization, as bariatric surgery is low priority for elective ICU beds.

  • Raise direct ICU admission threshold to BMI ≥ 50 (with major comorbidities or severe OSA without CPAP).
  • Pilot: Patients BMI 50-55 managed in ARCU, step down to HDU, with ICU bed on standby.

* Participation in the pilot is voluntary pending cancellation data review.

Surgical Site Infection (SSI) Updates

New ICN guidelines on induction antibiotics to achieve bactericidal concentration.

Initial Dose

Administer within 30 minutes before incision. Must be infused completely prior to incision.

Active Infection

If current agent covers prophylaxis: extra dose within 30 mins. If insufficient: add coverage. Consider Vancomycin for MRSA.

Redosing Rules

Required if excessive blood loss (>1,500 ml) OR at interval of 2x half-life (e.g., Augmentin half-life 1hr = redose every 2-3hrs, max 3 doses).

* Document in notes if surgical team insists on NOT providing induction antibiotics.

📋 M&M Reporting

QR codes available at back of ACIS computers.

  • Perioperative death (within 48 hrs)
  • Code Red/Blue/Arrest on OT floor
  • Arrest during peripheral anaesthesia
  • Unanticipated ICU admissions
  • Morbidity/mortality warranting consult
  • Surgery aborted after anaesthesia given
